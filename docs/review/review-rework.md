The Sessions page has evolved into something much more than a simple “walk list.” Its design language now sits at the intersection of:

a walking journal,
a route memory system,
and a lightweight exploration dashboard.

That direction aligns extremely well with the overall app philosophy described in the project docs — especially the emphasis on:

offline-first recording,
route replay,
review,
and outdoor-focused calm UX.
Overall Design Direction

The Sessions page is designed to feel:

visual,
calm,
memory-oriented,
and outdoors-inspired,

rather than:

analytical,
dense,
or fitness-obsessed.

This follows the visual principles established in the style guide:

card-based layout,
generous spacing,
rounded containers,
soft tonal hierarchy,
and strong readability outdoors.

The page intentionally prioritises:

experiences over statistics.
High-Level Layout Structure

The page is composed of four primary sections:

Section	Purpose
Header	Identity + top-level actions
Perspective Switcher	Different ways of viewing sessions
Weekly Summary Card	Momentum and activity overview
Session Feed	Rich visual memory cards
1. Header Area

At the top:

Sessions
Your walking adventures

This immediately frames the screen emotionally:

not “activity history,”
not “recorded workouts,”
but personal journeys.

The map background behind the sheet subtly reinforces:

place,
geography,
exploration,
without overwhelming the UI.

This follows the app’s “terrain and nature” visual identity.

2. Perspective Switcher

The segmented control:

Recent · Routes · Nearby

is one of the most important conceptual additions.

These are not merely “filters.”
They are:

perspectives on walking history.
Recent

Chronological memory feed.

Routes

Groups repeated sessions by reusable walking route/circuit.

This directly reflects the architectural distinction between:

Sessions (individual outings),
and Routes (reusable paths).
Nearby

Spatially organised memories based on current location.

This reinforces the app’s place-oriented identity.

3. Weekly Summary Card

This is the page’s visual anchor.

It transforms the screen from:

a list of walks

into:

an ongoing walking lifestyle

The card summarises:

total distance,
session count,
time outdoors,
calories (optional lifestyle metric),
and rolling weekly trend bars.
Why Weekly Instead Of Daily

This was a very strong UX decision.

Daily activity charts:

create empty gaps,
feel fitness-focused,
and introduce guilt.

Weekly aggregation:

feels calmer,
more natural,
and better aligned with casual walking behaviour.

The weekly bars provide:

continuity,
rhythm,
and momentum,
without turning the app into a gamified tracker.
4. Session Cards

The session cards are now designed as:

visual memory snapshots.

This is probably the most important evolution of the page.

Card Structure
Top Area

Date grouping:

Today
Wed, 7 May
Sun, 4 May

This preserves chronology and narrative memory.

Photo Strip

Large horizontally scrollable thumbnails dominate the card visually.

This is intentional.

Photos:

identify walks instantly,
trigger memory,
differentiate repeated routes,
and add emotional richness.

This aligns with the review architecture where photos are treated as:

timeline events attached to the route.

Multiple photos:

scroll horizontally,
show a “+3” overlay for additional images,
and make sessions feel alive without opening detail screens.
Route Preview

A tiny simplified route silhouette appears at the bottom-left.

This gives each session:

recognisable shape,
place identity,
and visual uniqueness.

Importantly:
the design moved away from emphasising elevation here.

That was a very strong decision.

Why Elevation Was Reduced

Elevation:

is analytical,
terrain-specific,
detail-oriented.

But Sessions is intended to be:

overview and memory.

Elevation fits much better inside:

review,
replay,
training analysis,
and detailed session screens.

The overview feed benefits more from:

photography,
route identity,
and chronology.
Metadata Row

Only lightweight metadata remains visible:

14.9 km · 4h 12m · 612m

This keeps the cards:

glanceable,
clean,
and non-overwhelming.

The details still exist deeper in the review experience.

Visual Tone

The page intentionally avoids:

dense stat grids,
heavy telemetry,
fitness gamification,
aggressive achievement systems.

Instead it leans into:

calmness,
exploration,
and personal history.

This directly matches the app’s stated tone:

Calm
Natural
Reliable
Practical

Relationship To The Wider App

The Sessions page sits at the centre of the app’s lifecycle:

Explore
→ Record / Follow
→ Review
→ Sessions archive
→ Explore again

This mirrors the architectural stages described across:

recording,
reviewing,
and replaying.
Most Important Design Insight

The Sessions page is no longer:

“storage.”

It has become:

“walking memory.”

That single shift changes:

hierarchy,
imagery,
layout,
and information density.

And it differentiates the product very strongly from generic fitness trackers.
